Wealth deserves better architecture.

Over $1 trillion in Canadian wealth is passing between generations this decade. And history tells us most of it won't survive the transition.

Not because of bad investments. Not because of poor returns. Because nobody built the infrastructure to hold it all together.

The wealth management industry has spent decades perfecting the science of portfolio performance. Returns, benchmarks, alpha, risk-adjusted metrics. Billions have been poured into platforms that can tell you exactly how a portfolio performed last quarter, down to the basis point.

And yet.

The trust deed that governs a family's $40 million holding company? It's in a shared drive somewhere. Maybe. The ownership structure connecting three generations across eight entities? It lives in an advisor's head. The governance decisions that shaped a family's wealth strategy over the past decade? Scattered across emails, meeting notes, and spreadsheets that nobody can find when it matters.

The industry built tools for the numbers. Nobody built the system for everything the numbers sit inside.

The real architecture of wealth

Complex family wealth doesn't live in a portfolio. It lives in a web of legal entities. Trusts nested inside holding companies. Operating businesses linked to partnerships. Personal accounts threaded through corporate structures. Each with its own governance, its own compliance obligations, its own documentation.

In Canada, this complexity is structural. Provincial regulations shift by entity type. CSA, OSC, and CIRO each impose distinct requirements. A family with a discretionary trust, two holding companies, and cross-border beneficiaries doesn't just need a dashboard. They need an operating system that understands how all of it connects.

Today, the advisory firms that manage these families (Investment Counsel firms, Multi-Family Offices) do it with spreadsheets, PDFs, and manual data pulls from six different portals before a single client meeting. Not because they lack sophistication. Because no one has built them anything better.

The family office software that does exist was built for family offices themselves, not for the advisory firms that serve dozens of them simultaneously. Different buyers. Different workflows. Different compliance regimes. Different market entirely. And none of it was designed for Canadian regulations.

What we believe

Wealth management has a missing layer.

Between the portfolio management systems below and the client relationships above, there is no system of record for the structures, governance, and institutional knowledge that actually determine whether wealth endures. Mozaiik is that layer.

Architecture matters more than features.

Every existing platform is built account-first, starting with investment accounts and aggregating upward. Mozaiik is built entity-first, starting with the family's legal entities and mapping the ownership relationships, governance structures, and documentation that connect them. This is not a feature. It is a fundamentally different foundation. It cannot be retrofitted.

The soft architecture is what breaks.

90% of wealth transfer failures are caused by fractured decision-making, unclear governance, undocumented family values, and scattered institutional knowledge. Not poor investment returns. The industry has no system for any of it.

The advisor's institutional memory should not be perishable.

When a lead advisor retires, changes firms, or is simply unavailable, decades of context about a family's structures, decisions, values, and strategy shouldn't walk out the door with them. A firm's knowledge about its families should compound over time, not reset.

Canadian fiduciaries deserve purpose-built infrastructure.

Not US platforms adapted for a different regulatory environment. Not family office tools repurposed for a different buyer. Infrastructure built from the ground up for the way Canadian advisory firms actually work. Entity by entity. Regulation by regulation. Family by family.

What we're building

Mozaiik is the first wealth operating system purpose-built for Canadian Investment Counsel firms and Multi-Family Offices.

It is a persistent system of record. Not just for portfolios, but for the entity structures, ownership relationships, governance decisions, compliance workflows, and qualitative dimensions of family wealth that no other platform captures. Estate planning context. Trust arrangements. Family governance. Succession planning. Values alignment.

Mozaiik sits above custodians and portfolio management systems as the firm's institutional memory: the single source of truth that holds together everything that determines whether wealth actually transfers successfully across generations.

The platform where an advisor opens their screen Monday morning and their entire week is organized.

Where every family's full picture is visible in one place. Entities, documents, compliance status, governance history. Where answering "what is this family actually worth, across everything?" takes seconds instead of hours.

Why now

Four forces are converging that make this moment inevitable.

Regulatory pressure is accelerating

In December 2025, Canadian regulators reviewed 105 firms and found 70% of Portfolio Managers had inadequate documentation. CSA, OSC, and CIRO are tightening simultaneously. Compliance is no longer a best practice. It is existential.

The Great Wealth Transfer is underway

The largest intergenerational wealth transfer in Canadian history is creating more trusts, more holding companies, more entity complexity every year. The problem is compounding.

Client expectations have shifted

Families managing wealth across multiple entities expect consolidated visibility, real-time access, and institutional-grade governance. The gap between what clients expect and what advisors can deliver is widening.

AI changes what's possible

AI built on top of structured, entity-aware data can surface insights no advisor could find manually. AI built on top of spreadsheets and PDFs is just a faster way to search through chaos. The architecture has to come first.

We are not building another dashboard. We are not building another reporting tool. We are not building software for software's sake.

We are building the system that a fiduciary reaches for first, and the one they cannot imagine working without.

Stewardship over sales.
Clarity over complexity.
Judgment over automation.

This is what wealth deserves. This is what we're building.
